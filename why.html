Hear to Know

1

What is Hearing Loss?

Hearing loss can be conductive (affecting the outer or middle ear), sensorineural (due to inner ear or auditory nerve damage), or mixed (a combination of both). Sensorineural loss is often permanent, caused by aging or noise exposure, while conductive loss may be treatable.

2

Possible Causes and Symptoms

Hearing loss can be caused by factors such as earwax buildup, aging, loud noise exposure, a perforated eardrum, or illnesses like meningitis. Symptoms include difficulty hearing clearly, frequently asking for repetition, needing higher volumes, and stressed from concentrating on listening.

3

Preventions and Protection

Protect your hearing by avoiding sounds over 85 decibels, using ear protection in noisy environments, and having regular hearing checks to prevent damage and maintain hearing health.

4

Hearing Tests and Diagnosis

An audiogram and speech tests assess hearing loss and comprehension, while online hearing tests offer quick, preliminary results. Clinical evaluations by professionals provide accurate assessments and treatment plans.

5

Treatment Options

Treatments for hearing loss include surgeries, cochlear implants, and hearing aids like BTE, ITE, CIC, and bone-conduction devices. Modern hearing aids amplify sound and offer features like noise cancellation and smartphone connectivity, improving communication and quality of life.

6

Rehabilitation and Support

Rehabilitation services, including speech therapy, lip-reading classes, and psychological support, help individuals enhance communication, understand conversations through visual cues, and manage emotional challenges, improving overall well-being and quality of life.

7

The Science Behind Hearing

Hearing starts as sound waves enter the ear, vibrating the eardrum and tiny bones in the middle ear. These vibrations reach the cochlea in the inner ear, where hair cells convert them into electrical signals sent to the brain for interpretation.

8

Call to Action

It’s crucial to prioritize your hearing health. Early detection of hearing loss can lead to better outcomes and improved quality of life.
