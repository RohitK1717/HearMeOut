Types of hearing loss


Conductive


Conductive hearing loss happens when something blocks or reduced the flow of sound in the outer or middle ear, leading to make soundwaves to unable to pass efficiently through the outer or middle hear to reach the inner ear. This can be caused by issused such as earwax buildup, fluid in the ear, or damage to the eardrum. It can occur in one ear (unilateral) or both ears (bilateral).



Types of hearing loss


Sensorineural


Sensorineural hearing loss happens when there is damage to the inner ear or the nerve that sends sound signals to the brain. This type of hearing loss is typically due to aging or prolonged exposure to loud noises. People with sensorineural hearing loss may find higher-pitched sounds muffled and may experience symtoms such as difficulty hearing, or ringing in the ears.



Types of hearing loss


Mixed hearing loss happens when someone has both Sensorineural and Conductive hearing loss. This means that there is a problem with both the outer or middle ear and the inner ear or hearing nerve. This can be caused by ear infections, trauma, aging, or exposure to loud noises. Mixed hearing loss can be partially treated or improved by it is generally not reversible.
